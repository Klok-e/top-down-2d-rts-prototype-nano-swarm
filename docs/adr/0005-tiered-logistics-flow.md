# Tiered logistics flow with terminal consumers

The material chain has three directed legs — deposit → source stockpile (worker), source stockpile → sink stockpile (hauler), and sink stockpile → terminal (hauler). Production facilities and chargers are terminal consumers with local input buffers; both receive minerals only from same-swarm sink stockpiles. Workers and haulers transfer cargo gradually at both ends of movement. Sink stockpiles remain shared bidirectional staging nodes: sinks in leg 2, sources in leg 3.

Haulers rank terminal work by urgency, then waiting-time fairness, then route cost; critical defender sustain outranks immediately startable production, while aging prevents permanent starvation. Emergency charger demand is bounded by minerals needed to fully recharge defenders committed to that charger; unused charger capacity remains ordinary refill demand. Worker and hauler assignments reserve exact source quantities and destination capacity without moving resources, allowing partial loads. Mineral custody changes only during gradual transfer, in-transit cargo remains part of swarm resources, invalid destinations cause rerouting or physical return to a compatible stockpile, and destruction of a loaded nanobot loses its cargo.

The `sink_stockpile_demand_system` auto-plans sink capacity from production demand inside painted Build Zones, but planned sink stockpiles are shared infrastructure rather than facility-bound inventories. Chargers remain direct-delivery terminals in Defend Zones instead of owning dedicated stockpiles, preserving logistics pressure without allowing stockpile-to-charger teleportation.
